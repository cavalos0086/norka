CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

My first research method for this project was contextual inquiry. Visiting the store allowd me to know more about the brand and talk to the customers about their preference to shop at this bookstore. I used this data to keep in mind their brand for the re-design of their site.

WHY STRAND?

  • Good selection on new/used books
  • "I can sell my own books"
  • Variety of genres
  • Employees assistance to customers
  • Reasonable/cheap prices
  • Staff capable and able to help
  • Book for all ages

COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

After doing the contextual inquiry I conducted competitive and comparative analysis. Using Numbers I created charts for comparison and easier understanding. This analysis helped me to get familiar with the 3 most relevant competitors. I used the result to see in which ways the flow of the competitors website could be improved


These 3 most relevant competitors were mentioned by the people I talked to in the store while I conducted Contextual Inquiry.

PERSONAS

After processing the research, I got 3 personas to stay focused on their needs and expectations as users. I summarized all their needs and what to avoid based on their past experiences.

My design had to meet the goals of the 3 personas, mostly for Daria since she was the main user I had to design for.

CARD SORTING

Following the research, I did card sorting using print outs of 100 actual products from the store. I had 8 participants organized these products in a sequence order that makes better sense for them. This cart sorting method helped me to define the categories of the products and how users would look for them.

The first 4 participants did open-card-sorting allowing me to see and understand what product categoiers were expected by users and what other features they needed during the buying process.
The other 4 participants were asked to do closed-card sorting; giving them specific product categories they had to organize the store products. This method helped me to test the categories I obtained during the open-card-sorting.

SITEMAP

Followed by compiling the outcomes of the cardsorting, I created a sitemap using Omnigraffle to have the main and subcategories of the potential website.
