DESIGN STUDIO

After doing feature prioritization we applied the design studio method. Using paper and a sharpie, we time-boxed ourselves to generate many ideas for the potential design. After an hour, we compiled our most appealing ideas to come up with a low fidelity wireframes.

WIREFRAMES

Our first step was to create low-fidelity wireframes to think of the layout of the app. We divided the main wireframes and digitalized them in Sketch. We used these wireframes to create the first prototype.

1
Donation progress:

Users are able to see the impact of their donations

2
About:

Detailed information about the Organization and their goals

3
Support this cause:

One tap away to start the donation process

4
Detailed information:

Users are informed about how their donation will be spent

5
Microdonation capability:

Users are able to input amount to donate

6
Messages:

Message capability within the app

After running the first user testing rounds, we were able to make changes and add more information to these low-fidelity wireframes

PROTOTYPE

Seeing is different than imagining, we finally had a low fidelity prototype in Invision. We used this prototype in user testing, we were able to see the interaction between users and interface

USER TESTING

Our prototype was ready in Invision. We decided to understand our product from the customer's perspective through user testing. We had users go through different tasks, we were able to see them struggle and misunderstand some parts of the design.
